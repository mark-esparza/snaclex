What SnaCleX is

SnaCleX is a research-only tool for analyzing public protein structures and chemical data. It has no user accounts, logins, or profiles, and it does not ask for your name, email, or any personal or clinical information.

What we collect

  • The scientific identifiers you enter — PDB IDs, protein names, chemical names/CIDs, and screening lists. These are sent to the server to run your analysis and to query the upstream data sources below.
  • Standard request metadata — your IP address and basic request details are processed transiently to serve responses and to enforce rate limits that keep the service available. We do not use this to identify or track individuals.

SnaCleX sets no cookies and uses no third-party advertising or analytics trackers.

Third-party data sources

To analyze a structure, the server queries public scientific services on your behalf. Your query terms are shared with whichever services are needed:

  • RCSB Protein Data Bank — structure files, metadata, and search.
  • PubChem (NCBI) — chemical properties and 3D conformers.
  • ChEMBL (EMBL-EBI) — drug/bioactivity cross-references.
  • Pfam / InterPro (EMBL-EBI) — family alignments for conservation.

Each service is governed by its own terms and privacy practices — see NOTICE.

Retention

Analysis results are computed per request and held only in a small, bounded in-memory cache to speed up repeat lookups; they are not written to a database and are lost when the service restarts. Reports you export (.txt) are generated in your browser session.

Your choices

Because there are no accounts and no persistent personal records, there is no profile to access or delete. Simply stop using the tool to stop sending data. Questions can be directed to the project maintainer via the repository.
